# アナーハタ・ナーダ（anāhata-nāda）

## 区分
**区分A — HYP確認済み**（HYP 第4章「On Samadhi」ナーダ関連節）

## 読み
アナーハタ・ナーダ

## 語源
anāhata（アナーハタ / 打たれざる・無打）＋ nāda（ナーダ / 音・響き）。
直訳は「打たれることなく生じる音」。内体に自然発生する内的音。

## 原典記述（HYP 第4章）

> Adinatha propounded 1 1/4 crore methods of trance, and they are all extant. Of these, the hearing of the anahata nada is the only one, the chief, in my opinion.

> Sitting with Mukta āsana and with the Sambhavi Mudra, the Yogi or Yogini should hear the sound inside his or her right ear, with collected mind.

> The ears, the eyes, the nose, and the mouth should be closed and then the clear sound is heard in the passage of the Susumna which has been cleansed of all its impurities.

> In all the Yogas, there are four states: (1) arambha or the preliminary, (2) Ghata, or the state of a jar, (3) Parichaya (known), (4) nispatti (consummate).

*出典: 05_Sources/HYP_Svatmarama_fulltext.md — Chapter 4, On Samadhi*

### アーランバ・アヴァスター（Arambha Avastha — 初段階）
> When the Brahma granthi (in the heart) is pierced through by Pranayama, then a sort of happiness is experienced in the vacuum of the heart, and the anahat sounds, like various tinkling sounds of ornaments, are heard in the body.

> In the arambha, a Yogi's or Yogini's body becomes divine, glowing, healthy, and emits a divine smell. The whole of his or her heart becomes void.

*出典: 05_Sources/HYP_Svatmarama_fulltext.md — Chapter 4, Arambha Avastha*

### ガタ・アヴァスター（Ghata Avastha — 第二段階）
> In the second stage, the airs are united into one and begun moving in the middle channel. The Yogi's or Yogini's posture becomes firm, and he or she becomes wise like a god.

> By this means the Visnu knot (in the throat) is pierced which is indicated by highest pleasure experienced, and then the Bheri sound (like the beating of a kettle drum) is evolved in the vacuum in the throat.

*出典: 05_Sources/HYP_Svatmarama_fulltext.md — Chapter 4, The Ghata Avastha*

### パリチャヤ・アヴァスター（Parichaya Avastha — 第三段階）
> In the third stage, the sound of a drum is known to arise in the Sunya between the eyebrows, and then the Vayu goes to the Mahasunya, which is the home of all the siddhis.

> When the Rudra granthi is pierced, and the air enters the seat of the Lord (the space between the eyebrows), then the perfect sound like that of a flute is produced.

> The union of the mind and the sound is called the Raja-Yoga. The (real) Yogi or Yogini becomes the creator and destroyer of the universe, like God.

*出典: 05_Sources/HYP_Svatmarama_fulltext.md — Chapter 4, The Parichaya Avastha*

### ナーダの修習について
> The sound which a muni hears by closing his or her ears with his or her fingers, should be heard attentively, till the mind becomes steady in it.

> In the beginning, the sounds heard are of great variety and very loud; but, as the practice increases, they become more and more subtle.

> In the first stage, the sounds are surging, thundering like the beating of kettle drums and jingling ones. In the intermediate stage, they are like those produced by conch, Mridanga, bells, etc. In the last stage, the sounds resemble those from tinklets, flute, Vina, bee, etc.

> Mind is the master of the senses, and the breath is the master of the mind. The breath in its turn is subordinate to the laya (absorption), and that laya depends on the nada.

*出典: 05_Sources/HYP_Svatmarama_fulltext.md — Chapter 4, On Samadhi*

## 解説
HYP第4章の中心実践。アーディナータ（シヴァ）が説いたとされる1億2,500万の瞑想法の中から、Svātmārāmaが「最も重要なもの」として選んだのがこのアナーハタ・ナーダの聴取。
[Mukta Āsana（Siddhāsana）](siddhasana.md)と[Śāmbhavī Mudrā](sambhavi_mudra.md)の姿勢で右耳の内部の音を集中した心で聴く。

**四段階のアヴァスター（Avastha）：**

| 段階 | 名称 | 特徴 | ナーダの音 |
|---|---|---|---|
| 第1 | Ārambha（初段階） | ブラフマ・グランティ穿通・心臓の空洞に幸福感 | 装飾品の鈴音 |
| 第2 | Ghaṭa（瓶の状態） | ヴィシュヌ・グランティ穿通・姿勢の安定 | 太鼓の音（Bheri） |
| 第3 | Paricaya（習熟） | ルドラ・グランティ穿通・眉間の空洞に音 | 笛の音 |
| 第4 | Niṣpatti（成就） | （完全なサマーディ） | （記述なし） |

心→感覚を支配し、呼吸→心を支配し、ラヤ（吸収）→呼吸を支配し、ナーダ→ラヤを支配するという構造がHYPの核心。
